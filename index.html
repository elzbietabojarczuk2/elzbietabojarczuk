Methodology

The SBDAGF

Small Business Digital Accessibility and Growth Framework

v3.03 · May 2026 · CC BY 4.0

A research-backed, open-access UX methodology that integrates accessibility compliance, conversion optimization, and human-centered design into a replicable system that non-experts can follow.

Download the Practitioner Checklist PDF · SBDAGF
36.2M Small businesses in the U.S.
45.9% Of American workers employed by small businesses
88% Won't return after a bad digital experience

5 Core Principles

01

Accessibility-First Architecture

WCAG 2.1 Level AA success criteria embedded as design constraints at wireframing stage — before any development begins. Accessible by construction.

02

Rural & Underserved-Community Calibration

Distinct protocols for connectivity-constrained design, lower-digital-literacy audiences, localized persona development, and modified research instruments.

03

Data-Grounded Decision Making

Every design decision validated through empirical evidence — hypothesis-driven design, funnel analytics, usability validation, and outcome measurement.

04

Replicability by Non-Experts

Documented as a step-by-step practitioner curriculum for SBDC advisors, small-business owners, marketing professionals, and educators.

05

AI-Augmented Implementation

Leverages current AI platforms — Lovable, Cursor, Claude, v0, Bolt.new — to make professional-quality design accessible to non-technical users. A working methodology, not theory.

Two-Layer Architecture

Layer 1

Practitioner Research & Design

For UX practitioners, SBDC advisors, or trained small-business owners

Research → Design → Validate → Compliance → Publish
Layer 2

Small-Business Implementation

For small-business owners — no coding knowledge needed

Select → Customize → Deploy → Monitor → Optimize

5-Phase Practitioner Workflow

01

Research & Diagnosis

Funnel mapping, root-cause categorization, severity assessment, user interviews, and research synthesis.

02

Accessibility-First Design

Pre-validated contrast ratios, accessible typography, component libraries with ARIA specs, proper page structure.

03

Hypothesis-Driven Solutions

Testable hypotheses, 2–3 design variants, prioritized by Impact × Feasibility × Compliance.

04

Validation Before Development

Usability tests with 5–8 participants measuring task completion, time on task, error rate, and UMUX-Lite satisfaction.

05

Compliance Verification

Automated accessibility scans (zero Critical/Serious issues), keyboard-only navigation, screen reader test, SBDAGF Checklist.

National Impact

A public good, at national scale.

The SBDAGF is engineered for one purpose: to give the United States' small businesses — especially in rural and underserved communities — a cost-effective, accessibility-first path to compliant, high-converting digital products, disseminated through the federal SBDC network. Its importance is measured against documented national data.

An open-access endeavor — beyond any single employer

The framework's outputs — a published methodology, a practitioner curriculum, open design specifications, and rural-UX benchmark research — are public goods. Each adoption by a practitioner, an SBDC advisor, an educator, or a business owner extends national benefit independently, at near-zero marginal cost, and without requiring any direct engagement.
