Accessibility

Accessibility Statement

Conformance target: WCAG 2.1 Level AA · Last reviewed 26 May 2026

Accessibility is the core of my practice, so this site is held to the same standard I design to professionally. It is built to conform with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at Level AA.

Measures taken

  • Semantic HTML5 landmarks (header, nav, main, footer) and a logical heading hierarchy.
  • A "skip to main content" link and full keyboard operability with visible focus indicators.
  • ARIA roles and labels on navigation, lists, icons, and interactive controls; decorative graphics marked aria-hidden.
  • Color contrast meeting or exceeding the 4.5:1 (text) and 3:1 (large text / UI) AA thresholds.
  • Respect for prefers-reduced-motion — animations are disabled for users who request reduced motion.
  • Responsive, reflowable layout down to 320px with no loss of content or function; tap targets ≥ 44px.
  • Text resizing without breakage and content not conveyed by colour alone.

Assessment approach

The site is evaluated with automated tooling (axe DevTools) and manual keyboard-only and screen-reader checks. Accessibility is treated as a design constraint from the wireframe stage — the same accessibility-first principle that anchors the SBDAGF methodology.

Feedback

If you encounter an accessibility barrier, please email elzbieta.bojarczuk2@gmail.com and I will respond promptly and work to resolve it.
